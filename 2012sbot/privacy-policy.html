§ P01

Overview & Scope

This Privacy Policy describes how 2012's Bot (the "Bot") encounters, handles, and relates to information during its operation on the Discord platform. It applies to all users — server owners, administrators, moderators, and regular members — who interact with the Bot in any capacity.

The Developer — 2012hhh2012 — is a private individual, not a corporation, LLC, or commercial entity. This policy is provided in genuine good faith and accurately reflects the actual operation of the Bot to the best of the Developer's knowledge.

By using the Bot, you acknowledge that you have read and accept the data practices described in this Privacy Policy. If you do not accept these practices, you must remove the Bot from your server and cease all use.

This Privacy Policy should be read alongside the Terms of Service, which governs all other aspects of your use of the Bot.

§ P02

Information the Developer May Access

In the normal course of operating a Discord bot, the Developer may have incidental access to the following categories of information. This is information Discord makes available to any bot that has been granted access to a server:

Discord User IDs Numeric identifiers assigned by Discord. Used solely for command processing, permission management, and per-user configuration. Not linked to real-world identity by the Developer.
Server (Guild) IDs Numeric identifiers for servers where the Bot is active. Used for per-server configuration and operational context.
Channel IDs Identifiers for channels in which the Bot is active or responds. Used for routing responses and optional per-channel configuration.
Message Content Only when you directly invoke a command or trigger directed at the Bot. The Bot does not passively monitor, read, or log all messages in a server or channel.
Command Interaction Data Which commands are used and at what times. Used for debugging, error logging, and performance monitoring. Not linked to personal identity beyond a Discord User ID.
Role & Permission Data Server roles and permissions, only as needed to determine whether a user is authorized to use certain Bot features.

What We Do Not Collect

  • Real names, email addresses, phone numbers, or any personally identifiable information beyond Discord User IDs.
  • IP addresses, device information, or browser data (these are handled exclusively by Discord).
  • Payment information of any kind.
  • Messages in channels where the Bot is not directly invoked.
  • Private/direct messages between users.

The Developer does not intentionally collect, sell, trade, broker, or share personal data beyond what is strictly and minimally necessary for the Bot to function.

§ P03

AI Model Providers — Third-Party Data Processing

Critical Disclosure — AI Data Handling

This Bot uses artificial intelligence features powered by third-party AI model providers. These may include, but are not limited to, services such as OpenAI, Anthropic, Google DeepMind, Mistral, Cohere, or similar providers. When you interact with any AI-powered feature of the Bot, your inputs — including message content, prompts, and surrounding conversational context — are transmitted over the internet to these external providers for processing and response generation.

What the Developer Cannot Control

The Developer — 2012hhh2012 — exercises zero control over how AI model providers receive, store, process, use, retain, or share data submitted through their APIs. Specifically, the Developer has no visibility or authority over:

  • Whether your inputs and prompts are used to train, fine-tune, evaluate, or otherwise improve AI models.
  • How long your prompts and interaction history are retained by the AI provider.
  • What encryption standards or security measures the AI provider applies to your data in transit and at rest.
  • Whether your data is shared with sub-processors, affiliates, research partners, or other third parties by the AI provider.
  • Whether your data is subject to government requests, law enforcement subpoenas, or legal orders received by the AI provider.
  • How the AI provider handles data breaches, security incidents, or unauthorized access events involving your data.
  • The geographic location(s) where your data is stored or processed by the AI provider.

No Developer Liability for AI Data

All data processed by AI model providers is subject exclusively to those providers' own privacy policies, terms of service, and data processing agreements — not this policy. The Developer bears absolutely no responsibility and accepts no liability whatsoever for any data collection, retention, misuse, unauthorized access, disclosure, breach, or any other privacy-related incident arising from the handling of data by AI providers.

By using the Bot's AI features, you acknowledge and accept this third-party data processing and the inherent risks thereof.

⚠ Important Warning

Do not submit sensitive, confidential, personally identifiable, proprietary, or legally protected information to the Bot's AI features. This includes but is not limited to: full names combined with sensitive context, financial details, health or medical information, passwords or credentials, private communications, confidential business information, or any data you would not want transmitted to an external AI service. The Developer cannot guarantee or influence how such information is processed, stored, or used by AI providers.

Finding Out More

To understand how AI providers handle your data, you are strongly encouraged to review their respective privacy policies. The identity of specific AI provider(s) in use may change over time as the Developer updates or changes the Bot's infrastructure, without obligation to notify users of such changes.

§ P04

Discord Platform Data

The Bot operates entirely within and upon Discord's platform. Discord Inc. independently collects, stores, and processes user data — including messages, metadata, IP addresses, device information, and behavioral analytics — in accordance with Discord's own Privacy Policy and Terms of Service.

The Developer does not control Discord's data practices, cannot access Discord's underlying infrastructure or databases, and is not responsible for Discord's data handling in any respect whatsoever.

  • Discord may log API calls made by the Bot, including timestamps, command types, and associated User and Server IDs.
  • Discord may retain interaction data independently of any data the Developer retains.
  • Discord's web client, mobile app, and other interfaces may collect additional data not visible to or controlled by the Developer.
Concerns about Discord's data collection, retention, or practices must be directed to Discord directly at discord.com/privacy. The Developer has no authority to act on Discord's behalf.
§ P05

Data Storage, Retention & Security

Any data retained by the Developer in connection with the Bot's operation — such as server configuration settings, per-user preferences, or operational error logs — is handled with reasonable precautions appropriate to the nature of the data and the Developer's capacity as a private individual.

Retention Principles

  • Data is retained only as long as operationally necessary.
  • Configuration data may be retained for the lifetime of the Bot's presence in a server.
  • Error and diagnostic logs are periodically cleared and not retained indefinitely.
  • Removing the Bot from your server will terminate its future access to your server's data. Some residual configuration data may be retained briefly before cleanup.

Security

No method of electronic storage or data transmission is 100% secure. While the Developer takes reasonable precautions to protect data, no absolute security guarantee can be made. Users interact with the Bot understanding and accepting this inherent risk of online services.

No Data Monetization

The Developer does not:

  • Sell, rent, trade, or otherwise monetize user data.
  • Operate a commercial database of user personal information.
  • Share user data with advertisers, data brokers, or marketing companies.
  • Use user data for any purpose other than the operation of the Bot.
§ P06

Cookies & Tracking Technologies

The Bot itself — as a Discord bot application — does not use cookies, web beacons, tracking pixels, fingerprinting, or any browser-based or device-based tracking technologies. There is no website operated by the Developer in connection with the Bot that independently tracks users.

Any tracking technologies encountered when using Discord's own platform — including Discord's web interface, desktop application, or mobile apps — are governed exclusively by Discord's own cookie and tracking policies, over which the Developer has no control or influence.

§ P07

Children's Privacy

The Bot is not directed to, and not intended for use by, individuals under the age of 13 (or such higher minimum age as required by applicable law in the user's jurisdiction — for example, 16 in certain EU member states under GDPR).

The Developer does not knowingly collect or retain personal information from children under these age thresholds. If the Developer becomes aware that personal information has been inadvertently collected from a child, that information will be promptly deleted to the extent the Developer is able.

If you have reason to believe that a minor is using the Bot in violation of this policy or of Discord's minimum age requirements, please contact the Developer immediately via Discord, and also report the matter to Discord directly.

Parents and guardians are encouraged to monitor their children's online activity and use of third-party bots and AI-powered services.
